Brandi Passante, Storage Wars Star, Gets $750 in Porn Video Lawsuit

 Posted on June 15, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  227 words  |  Aldo Pusey

Although Brandi Passante of Storage Wars was seeking $2.5 million in a lawsuit against Hunter Moore, former owner of the website, Is Anyone Up, alleging that he posted a fake porn video of her, she was only awarded $750 after a judge's ruling on Friday, June 21.

According to The Wrap, the A&E reality star alleged that Moore posted a fake porn video of her on his site, and was seeking $2.5 million in actual and exemplary damages, plus $5,250 in statutory damages.

On Friday, June 21, Judge James V. Selna ruled in Passante's favor, granting her motion for a default judgment. She received a small fraction of the amount she was seeking because the judge reportedly ruled that Passante provided "absolutely no support for this calculation of damages” and “a lack of evidentiary support.” 

In the lawsuit filed in October 2012, Passante charged Moore with false designation of origin, defamation, false light invasion of privacy, and disparagement.
