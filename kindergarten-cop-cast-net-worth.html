This unconventional arrangement allowed the trio to capitalize on the film’s success, resulting in substantial profits. “Twins” grossed over $216 million worldwide, cementing its place as one of Schwarzenegger’s highest-grossing movies to date. By taking a risk and prioritizing long-term returns over immediate paychecks, Arnold Schwarzenegger secured a substantial earning potential that extended far beyond the initial release.

Political Career

Arnold Schwarzenegger, known for his successful film career, also ventured into the world of politics. Running as a Republican candidate, Schwarzenegger leveraged his popularity and charisma to secure the position.

During his tenure as Governor, Schwarzenegger faced numerous challenges and implemented key policies that shaped California’s future. His focus on environmental issues led to the signing of the California Global Warming Solutions Act in 2006, which aimed to reduce greenhouse gas emissions and combat climate change. Additionally, Schwarzenegger was a strong advocate for education reform, addressing the shortcomings of the state’s education system throughout his term.

However, his political career also had financial and personal consequences. In order to fulfill his roles as both Governor and an actor, Schwarzenegger had to put his film career on hold, resulting in an estimated loss of $200 million in potential movie wages. Despite this setback, Schwarzenegger remained committed to public service and dedicated his time to governing California.
