Surfer Mikala Jones Dead at 44 After Surfing Accident

 Posted on July 18, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  504 words  |  Martina Birk

Surfer Mikala Jones Dead at 44 After Surfing Accident.

Mikala Jones, a professional surfer, tragically passed away at the age of 44 after a devastating surfing accident.

His daughter, Isabella Jones, shared the heartbreaking news on Instagram, expressing her disbelief and sorrow.

She fondly remembered her father’s love for surfing and wished she could have one last hug with him.

Isabella also expressed her love for him and gratitude for being the best dad.

She expressed her longing for his presence and wished they could surf together again.

Despite the immense grief, Isabella found solace in believing that Mikala is now in a better place, reunited with loved ones.

Expressing her deep longing for her father, Isabella expressed her heartfelt desire for one more moment with him.

She expressed gratitude for the invaluable life lessons he taught her and for always being there.

Isabella wished that he could still be with them and assured him that he would always be in her thoughts.

She professed her love for him and thanked him for everything, acknowledging him as a legendary figure who will forever remain in her heart.

Mikala Jones, the renowned Hawaiian pro surfer, gained recognition as one of the most visually captivating surfers of the 21st century.

His talent for tube-riding across different locations worldwide earned him the title.

Mikala often captured his surfing experiences on his GoPro, offering a unique perspective from inside the barrels he conquered.

His sponsor, Matuse wetsuits, described Mikala as an intelligent and sophisticated individual.

According to Beach Grit, he possessed a street-smart and savvy nature while remaining humble.

Mikala was known for guarding secret surfing spots and maintained a private family life with residences in Haleiwa and Bali.

Despite his privacy, his personality, professionalism, and smooth style led him on jet-setting adventures each year in pursuit of exceptional photo opportunities.

Following Mikala’s tragic passing, numerous members of the surfing community expressed their condolences on social media.

Kelly Slater, an 11-time World Surf League champion, referred to Mikala as a “legend” in a comment on Isabella’s post.

Tia Blanco, a Puerto Rican surf star, shared her sadness and praised Mikala as one of the kindest individuals she had encountered in the surfing community.

She sent love and strength to Isabella during this difficult time.

Photographer Nathan Myers shared his own heartfelt tribute, accompanied by photos he had taken of Mikala.

Recalling their long history together, Nathan expressed his reluctance to bid farewell.

He recounted their first meeting 25 years ago on an editorial trip and the countless waves they shared.

Their families became intertwined, creating cherished memories that now resurfaced.

Nathan expressed gratitude for their recent time spent together, including surfing with their children and enjoying laughter-filled moments.

He expressed a shared anticipation for future adventures. Despite the loss, Nathan vowed to carry Mikala’s spirit and cherished memories with him.

He concluded his tribute by acknowledging Mikala as a true legend.
