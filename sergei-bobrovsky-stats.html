Know About Sergei Bobrovsky; Stats, Height, Trade, Wife, Contract

After some astounding stats with Jackets, Sergei Bobrovsky has agreed on a multi-million contract with his new NHL team. The contract is for seven years that will keep him at the club until 2026.

Russian professional ice hockey goaltender, Sergei Bobrovsky plays for NHL team, Florida Panthers. The former Flyers star is the Gold medalist of the 2014 World Championship in Belarus representing his nation, Russia.

Sergei Bobrovsky Bio & Family

The player was born as Sergei Andreyevich Bobrovsky on 20 September 1988 in Novokuznetsk, Russian SFSR, Soviet Union. His parents, Andrey and Larisa raised him. Sergei stands at a height of 6 feet 2 inches (188 cm) tall and weighs around 83 kg.

Career & Net Worth

Sergei was one player considered at the 2006 NHL draft but Russian players were not given a green signal. So, he went to play for Metallurg Novokuznetsk in Russia for four seasons and got a three-year staggering contract with Flyers on 6 May 2010.

Sergei Bobrovsky playing stats (Picture: NHL)

Two years later, Flyers had no option but to trade him to the Columbus Blue Jackets on 22 June 2012. The 2012/13 season was NHL lockout, so he had allied with KHL team, SKA Saint Petersburg. Likewise, a year later, his two years contract was worth $11.25 million on 1 July 2013.

After committing six incredible years at Jackets, he signed a seven-year contract worth $70 million contracts with the Panthers on 1 July 2019. A part of his contract also includes an annual average of $10 million.

Sergei Bobrovsky Wife

Besides his great stats at NHL league, he has the most caring wife as Olya Dorokova. It’s been over eight years that they cherish their beautiful matrimony after exchanging wedding vows on 16 August 2011.

Sergei Bobrovsky with wife, Olya during NHL Awards at T-Mobile Arena on 21 June 2017 in Las Vegas, Nevada (Picture: Zimbio)

Sergei is a complete family and besides his busy playing schedules, he enjoys spending time with his parents and wife.

Some Interesting Facts About Sergei Bobrovsky

  • In his NHL debut, he became the game’s first star in the first regular season game at Pittsburgh’s Consol Energy Center.
  • Sergei became the youngest goaltender to win a season-opening game for the Flyers at 22 years and 17 days.
  • He is the 2013 recipient of the NHL First All-Star Team.
  • The player has represented his nation at the 2008 Czech Republic, 2014 Belarus, 2015 Czech Republic, and 2016 Russia.
  • Sergei is also called by his nicknames like “Bob” and “The Number One Cop.”
